# 假目标/诱饵分类体系

This taxonomy defines the top-level structure for an "awesome decoys" knowledge base. It intentionally covers military dummy targets, signal decoys, cyber deception, historical ruses, and future autonomous systems under one shared vocabulary.

## 1. Working Definition

A dummy/decoy is any deliberately constructed object, signal, behavior, identity, system, or story that causes an observer, sensor, algorithm, weapon, intruder, or decision-maker to form a wrong belief or allocate resources incorrectly.

Related but distinct concepts:

- **Camouflage** - Reduces observability of a real object.
- **Concealment** - Prevents or delays observation.
- **Dummy target** - Creates a false object or asset.
- **Decoy** - Attracts observation, classification, engagement, or interaction.
- **Spoofing** - Falsifies a signal, identity, measurement, or protocol response.
- **Deception operation** - Coordinates multiple decoys, concealment measures, signals, and narratives to produce a larger false picture.

| Era | Typical Forms | Representative Questions |

| Emerging intelligent deception | Autonomous decoy platforms, adaptive signatures, decoy swarms, cyber-physical deception | How can decoys sense, adapt, move, and learn? |

## 3. Feature Dimension

### Physical / Entity

Physical decoys imitate objects or facilities:

- Tanks, aircraft, ships, artillery, missile launchers, radars, shelters, vehicles, bridges, depots, command posts, tents, and personnel-like objects.
- Common media include wood, canvas, metal frames, inflatable fabric, printed surfaces, composite shells, modular kits, and expendable robotic platforms.

### Signal

Signal decoys imitate detectable emissions or sensor returns:

- RF transmissions, radio nets, radar returns, radar cross-section augmentation, corner reflectors, Luneberg lenses, acoustic signatures, thermal sources, lighting, AIS/GPS-like signals, and synthetic returns.
- The core issue is consistency: a false object should not emit a signal story that contradicts its shape, location, temperature, movement, or role.

### Behavioral

Behavioral decoys imitate activity:

- Movement, maintenance, loading, refueling, launch preparation, patrol rhythm, convoy tempo, command-post traffic, lights, tire tracks, heat cycles, and communications schedules.
- Behavioral credibility often matters more than object fidelity when sensors observe over time.

### Cyber

Cyber decoys lure, delay, observe, or mislead intruders:

- Honeypots, honeynets, honeytokens, canary files, fake credentials, decoy services, deception endpoints, fake databases, bogus documents, false identities, and adversary-engagement environments.
- Cyber decoys are evaluated by interaction depth, detection of attacker tradecraft, containment, alert quality, and whether the attacker changes behavior.

### Narrative / Intelligence

Narrative decoys create false confirmation across sources:

- False orders of battle, controlled leaks, double-agent channels, misleading logistics movement, planted documents, fake procurement traces, and staged infrastructure.
- The aim is not only to create a false target, but to make the false target appear independently confirmed.

## 4. Physical Evolution Dimension

| Stage | Core Character | Typical Capabilities |
|---|---|---|
| Classic / manual | Human-built, visual, local | Fires, banners, wooden shapes, canvas covers, dummy camps, painted surfaces |
| Mechanical / deployable | Faster setup, reusable, mobile | Inflatable forms, foldable frames, towable platforms, heaters, reflectors, simple emitters |
| Informationized / networked | Programmable and coordinated | Remote control, RF generators, networked emitters, synchronized signatures, sensor-triggered activation |
| Intelligent / autonomous | Adaptive and behavior-rich | Unmanned movement, dynamic signatures, path planning, swarm coordination, closed-loop effects assessment |

## 5. Technology Lifecycle Dimension

### Design / Generation

- Target-signature modeling.
- Threat-sensor modeling.
- Optical, thermal, radar, RF, acoustic, and cyber-signature design.
- CAD and digital twins.
- Synthetic imagery and test datasets.
- Scenario and deception-story generation.

### Production / Manufacturing

- Inflatable fabric cutting and sealing.
- Lightweight frames and modular kits.
- Coatings, surface textures, printed skins, heaters, reflectors, RF payloads.
- Additive manufacturing and field repair.
- Low-cost autonomy, power, and communication modules.
- Cyber deception templates and deployable deception environments.

### Deployment / Operation

- Placement, spacing, concealment, timing, and activation.
- Integration with real forces, emissions control, logistics, and camouflage.
- Repositioning, operator workload, power supply, weather resistance, and recovery.
- Coherence of the false story across object, signal, behavior, and intelligence layers.

### Testing / Evaluation

- Sensor-facing tests using EO, IR, SAR, radar, RF, acoustic, and cyber telemetry.
- Red-team classification and attack trials.
- Cost-exchange analysis.
- BDA ambiguity measurement.
- Pattern-of-life analysis.
- Cyber engagement metrics.
- Closed-loop deception effects assessment.

### Source Type / Provenance

This dimension classifies **where knowledge about a decoy comes from**, by genre. It is orthogonal to the A/B/C/D quality tier: source *type* describes what an item is; the quality *tier* describes how much evidentiary weight it carries, and for which claim. The same vendor page can be Tier A for "this product exists and is sold" and Tier D for "it defeats SAR in combat."

Controlled vocabulary (used in `data/sources.csv`, field `source_type`):

- `peer_reviewed_journal` - 学术/同行评审期刊.
- `conference_paper` - 会议论文与论文集 (e.g., AIAA, IEEE Radar, SPIE, NATO STO symposia).
- `book` - 书籍、专著、官方战史、报告章节.
- `think_tank_report` - 智库/研究机构报告 (RAND, CSIS, FPRI, IISS, RUSI, CNAS).
- `gov_military_official` - 政府/军方官方文档：条令、军种院校出版社、CRS/GAO、官方经验总结、国会证词、DIU/DTIC.
- `military_professional_journal` - 军事专业期刊 (Military Review, Proceedings, Parameters, Joint Force Quarterly, War Room) - institutionally published, practitioner-facing.
- `trade_defense_media` - 专业国防/航空媒体 (Jane's, The War Zone, Defense News, Breaking Defense, Aviation Week, FlightGlobal, Army Recognition).
- `news_media` - 综合新闻媒体与时政杂志 (AP, Reuters, Washington Post, RFE/RL, NBC, Newsweek, Bulletin of the Atomic Scientists).
- `vendor_industry` - 厂商/产业资料：产品页、目录、市场报告、新闻稿.
- `analysis_blog` - 独立/个人分析与聚合 (Substack, specialist blogs, republishers).
- `reference_encyclopedic` - 百科/专家参考汇编 (Wikipedia, designation-systems.net, GlobalSecurity.org).
- `osint_social` - 一手开源情报与社媒：地理定位影像、卫星图、Telegram/X、战场视频、军事博主.
- `dataset_database` - 数据集/数据库 (conflict datasets, missile-threat databases, order-of-battle trackers).
- `institutional_reference` - 博物馆/档案/遗产机构 (Imperial War Museums, English Heritage, national archives).
- `standard_patent_spec` - 标准、专利、技术规范.

Type-to-tier tendencies (defaults only; always refine per claim):

| Source type | Usual tier | Best used for | Watch out for |
|---|---|---|---|
| `peer_reviewed_journal` / `book` | A | Theory, methods, validated history | Lag behind current events |
| `gov_military_official` | A | Doctrine, official lessons, requirements | Disputed BDA, advocacy framing |
| `think_tank_report` | A | Assessments, force analysis, cost-exchange | Institutional viewpoint |
| `military_professional_journal` | A/B | Practitioner concepts, campaign analysis | Author opinion vs. official position |
| `trade_defense_media` | B | Systems detail, named observations, imagery, dates | Specs sometimes from vendor claims |
| `news_media` | B | Event confirmation, quotes, timelines | Effect claims need corroboration |
| `vendor_industry` | A for existence / D for effect | Product taxonomy, specs-as-claimed | Performance and combat-effect claims |
| `analysis_blog` | C | Early framing, leads, niche technical notes | Provenance, single-author bias |
| `osint_social` | D until verified | Earliest/only signal on recent events | Geolocation, date, manipulation, context |

For decoy research specifically, source type also predicts which **lifecycle stage** an item informs best: peer-reviewed and conference work for design/evaluation; vendor and trade media for production and product taxonomy; news and OSINT for deployment and combat use; doctrine and think-tank work for operational concepts and cost-exchange.

## 7. Recommended Repository Axes

The project should let a reader enter the knowledge base from several directions:

- **By era** - ancient, industrial, world wars, Cold War, contemporary, emerging.
- **By feature** - physical, signal, behavioral, cyber, narrative.
- **By domain** - land, air, maritime, space, EMS, cyber, information.
- **By technology lifecycle** - design, manufacturing, deployment, evaluation.
- **By case** - searchable structured records in `data/cases.csv`.
- **By source** - curated source index in `references/sources.md`.
- **By problem** - airbase survivability, missile defense, C2 survivability, cyber intrusion detection, BDA uncertainty, cost imposition.
